Education & Training

My educational journey took an unconventional path, shaped by unexpected challenges that ultimately led me to discover my true passion for technology.

Hurricane Katrina's Impact

In 2005, Hurricane Katrina changed everything about my education. The storm destroyed our city and forced me to move between four different schools. All this moving around made me lose interest in school, and I stopped caring about the usual classroom experience.

The Turning Point

By age 16, I had reached a breaking point. The traditional educational system felt like more of a hindrance than a help to my learning. I was far more passionate about exploring computers and technology than sitting through classes that seemed irrelevant to my interests.

"I'll never forget the day I woke up and told my mother I wasn't going to school anymore and that she could punish me forever for all I care."

That pivotal conversation with my mother could have gone very differently. Instead of resistance, she showed remarkable understanding. After consulting with counselors and carefully considering my situation, she agreed to let me pursue an alternative path. With her support, I decided to leave traditional schooling behind and work toward my GED.

The decision proved to be transformative. Within just two months, I had successfully passed my GED, demonstrating that my struggles weren't with learning itself but with the traditional educational approach that simply didn't align with how I learned best.

Finding My Path at Louisiana Technical College

At 16 years old, I immediately enrolled at Louisiana Technical College, finding myself in classrooms alongside adult students who were serious about their education. For the first time in years, I was truly excited about learning. The college environment allowed me to dive deep into technology courses, finally providing the structured yet practical education I had been craving.

This experience taught me that school had been a distraction from my real passion for learning about computers. Once I broke free from the constraints of traditional education, I finally started enjoying my life and became even more passionate about my future. The unconventional path I took wasn't just about getting credentials—it was about finding an educational approach that matched my learning style and interests.

Looking back, leaving traditional high school was one of the best decisions I ever made. It allowed me to start my real education on my own terms, at my own pace, and in an environment where I could thrive. Sometimes the most valuable lessons come from having the courage to forge your own path, even when it goes against conventional wisdom.
