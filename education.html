Education & Training

My formal education, combined with continuous self-learning and certifications, has been instrumental in my cybersecurity journey.

I began my formal education at the University of New Orleans, where I pursued a degree in Computer Science. While the academic curriculum provided a solid foundation in programming principles, algorithms, and computer architecture, I found myself drawn to the specialized field of cybersecurityâ€”an area that wasn't extensively covered in the standard curriculum at that time.

To supplement my formal education, I immersed myself in self-directed learning. The internet became my classroom, with countless hours spent on forums, reading documentation, and experimenting with various security tools. This hands-on approach allowed me to develop practical skills that would later prove invaluable in my career.

Education in cybersecurity is never complete. The field evolves too rapidly for complacency, requiring a commitment to lifelong learning and constant adaptation.

One of the most significant milestones in my educational journey was earning the Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP) certification. This rigorous, hands-on certification challenged me to demonstrate practical penetration testing skills under pressure. Unlike many certification programs that focus primarily on theory, the OSCP required me to successfully compromise various systems in a controlled lab environment, culminating in a 24-hour practical exam. This experience not only validated my technical skills but also reinforced the importance of persistence and creative problem-solving in cybersecurity.

I've also pursued other industry certifications, including the Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) and Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH). Each certification program offered unique perspectives and methodologies, broadening my understanding of different aspects of cybersecurity.

Beyond formal certifications, I've found tremendous value in attending security conferences and workshops. Events like DEF CON, Black Hat, and DerbyCon have provided opportunities to learn from industry experts, discover emerging threats and technologies, and network with like-minded professionals. These conferences often feature practical workshops where I could gain hands-on experience with cutting-edge tools and techniques.

Contributing to open-source security projects has been another essential component of my educational journey. By examining, modifying, and contributing to frameworks like Metasploit and Kali Linux, I gained deeper insights into their inner workings while simultaneously giving back to the community.

As I transitioned into entrepreneurship with the founding of Vonahi Security, my education expanded to include business management, leadership, and innovation. Leading a cybersecurity company required developing skills beyond technical knowledge, including strategic planning, team building, and client relationship management.

I believe that education in cybersecurity is never complete. The field evolves too rapidly for complacency, requiring a commitment to lifelong learning and constant adaptation. This philosophy of continuous education has guided my professional development and remains central to how I approach new challenges in the ever-changing landscape of cybersecurity.
